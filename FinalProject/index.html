Hillsboro Beach

By Luana Goncalves

Hillsboro Beach is a quiet town with the landmark lighthouse overlooking the inlet. On this day, a storm was brewing out in the ocean on my walk. I could not stay long since the storm rolled within 30 minutes. The ocean and the sky both looked menacing, and all you can hear on the deserted beach was the waves crashing. If you want to avoid South Florida’s tourist rush, this is the beach to come to enjoy solidarity in peace. I like to come to reset and find inspiration. The background music used for this video is called “Simple Truth” by Jeff Broadbent.

Deerfield Beach

By Luana Goncalves

Deerfield Beach is the perfect the place if you want a lively beach experience. Deerfield Beach is my hometown and where I grew up pretty much all of my life. People playing volleyball, surfing or simply having big party amongst friends. It is definitely a great place to people watch. Although this is a local hotspot, it is flourishing with tourists as well. Towards the end of the video you will see a group feeding a flock of seagulls, which is a big no, no! Besides tourists, there is plenty scenery to observe. The background music used for this video is called “Sunrise Meditation” by Jeff Broadbent.

Boca Raton

By Luana Goncalves

There is so much to see at Boca Raton! There are pretty jetties to climb onto to overlook the ocean and a neat inlet to fish off from. You can different boats and yachts enter and leave the inlet. I like walking here because you go through stretches that are desolate but also populated, so there is always a lot to see! Some of the jetties can form pools where you can witness tiny marine life. It’s a great beach to escape to, you just have to have endurance to walk long distances. The background music used for this video is called “Anti Gravity” by Nick Tidy.
